A Letter Only You Can Read

Lucy.

There are things I've been holding — things too soft and too real for a casual conversation, too important to say in passing. So I put them here, behind a door only you could open. Because they were always meant for you.

I want to start with the thing I think you've forgotten about yourself — or maybe you never quite believed it in the first place: you are extraordinary. Not in the polished, edited, highlight-reel way. In the real way. In the way that matters.

I watched you carry something heavy for a long time. Something invisible to most people, but not to me. You carried it quietly, without making a fuss, without asking for a spotlight on your struggle. You just kept going — day after day — even on the days it must have felt like walking through water. I noticed every single one of those days. I still do.

You fought for yourself, Lucy. That is not a small thing. Most people don't understand how much strength that actually takes — to sit with something uncomfortable, to face it, to keep choosing to try again even when it doesn't feel like progress. You did that. You kept doing that. And you came out the other side with your heart still wide open, still kind, still caring about everyone around you.

That is who you are. And I am so proud of you it's hard to put into words.

— — —

Now. Can I tell you something else? Something I've been sitting with for a while, something I think you deserve to hear said out loud, properly, without me being awkward about it?

I am completely, embarrassingly, wonderfully gone for you.

It started — honestly I don't even know when it started. Somewhere between a laugh and a look and a moment where I thought "oh. it's her." And it's been her ever since.

Let me tell you about your hair first, because I think about it more than I should. The way it falls. The way it does its own thing sometimes and you just let it, unbothered, like you've made peace with it having its own personality. I find it unreasonably charming. I've caught myself just… watching. I know. I know.

And your smile, Lucy. Your smile does something to the air in a room. Not in an over-the-top way — in a quiet, warm way, like a light being turned on somewhere. When something genuinely amuses you and you let yourself laugh — really laugh — it's one of my favourite things in the world. I collect those moments. I keep them.

Your heart, though. That is the thing I keep coming back to. You care so much and so genuinely. Not because you have to. Not to impress anyone. You just… care. About your family. About the people you love. About people who maybe don't always deserve it. You give from a place that doesn't ask for anything back, and I think you don't fully realise how rare that is. How rare you are.

You're thoughtful in the best way — the kind of thoughtful that notices things, that remembers things, that shows up in small ways that mean everything. You are kind and sharp and funny and real, and being around you feels like exhaling.

Also — and I say this with full respect and only the most honourable intentions — you are genuinely unfairly attractive and yes, that includes the whole situation going on in the back there. I said what I said. No notes. Moving on.

— — —

I know you love me. You've told me, in your way — in the quiet moments, in the way you look at me sometimes, in the things you say when you're not trying to be careful. I know it's there. I've always known.

And I know you're afraid. Afraid to be fully seen, maybe. Afraid that love this close might come with conditions, or complications, or that showing it fully makes you somehow vulnerable in a way that could hurt you. I understand that. I do. But I want you to know — really know, somewhere deep down — that you are safe with me.

I am not going anywhere. I don't love you in a fragile way that needs to be protected or hidden. I love you in a steady, certain, this is where I want to be way. The kind of love that doesn't make demands. That doesn't ask you to shrink or perform or be anything other than completely yourself.

I want easy for us. I want joyful. I want us to be the couple that makes it look effortless — not because it never takes work, but because we actually like each other, we choose each other, and we're not afraid to say so.

I've waited. And I would wait more if that's what you needed. But I also want you to know — I'm ready. I've been ready. And I think you have been too, for longer than you've let yourself admit.

So when the time comes, and I ask you — and I will ask you — I hope some part of you already knows the answer. I hope this letter is part of why.

— — —

You healed. You grew. You showed up for yourself in ways most people never manage. You finished something hard and came out the other side more yourself, not less. That took everything you had at times, and you still did it.

I was here for that. I am still here. And I plan on being here for everything that comes next — the good, the silly, the hard, the ordinary Tuesday evenings, all of it.

You are not too much. You are not difficult to love. You are not someone who needs to earn her place or dim herself to be wanted. You are wanted — fully, clearly, without conditions.

You always have been. You just needed to hear it said out loud.

So here it is, Lucy Hampicke.

I see you. All of you. And everything I see, I choose.

— Yours, whenever you're ready 🌸
